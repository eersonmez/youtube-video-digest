The spelled-out intro to language modeling: building makemore

This educational content walks through constructing a simple character-level language model based on a dataset of names. Initially, the speaker builds a bi-gram model by counting occurrences of adjacent characters in the training data and normalizing these counts to form a probability distribution. This allows for iterative sampling to generate new text. The process is then transitioned to using a neural network approach. By one-hot encoding input characters and designing the network to output log-counts, the model learns probabilities through gradient descent, minimizing the negative log-likelihood loss function across the training set. The speaker demonstrates that this gradient-based method converges to results equivalent to the explicit counting method, highlighting the flexibility of neural networks even for simple tasks.
