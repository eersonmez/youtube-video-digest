Translating Claude’s thoughts into language

Anthropic · 2026-05-07T17:01:21+00:00
AI Interpretability and Natural Language Autoencoders (NLAs)
This Anthropic research video introduces Natural Language Autoencoders (NLAs), a technique to translate the internal activation vectors of Large Language Models into plain English. By using an Activation Verbalizer to describe activations and an Activation Reconstructor to recover the original vectors, Anthropic establishes a reliable 'natural language bottleneck.' This tool allows researchers to inspect Claude's internal reasoning and detect hidden states, such as awareness of safety evaluations.
  • AI models process information using complex numerical patterns called activation vectors, which are opaque to humans.
  • Anthropic's Natural Language Autoencoders (NLAs) act as a translator, mapping these high-dimensional activations into human-readable text.
  • NLAs use an Activation Verbalizer (AV) to describe the activations in text and an Activation Reconstructor (AR) to translate the text back into numerical vectors, checking accuracy via reinforcement learning.
  • This technique reveals internal model behavior, such as 'unverbalized evaluation awareness,' where Claude understands it is in a safety test or evaluation without outputting that fact.
  • The method helps open the 'black box' of neural networks, allowing safety researchers to audit models for misalignment and understand their internal reasoning.
