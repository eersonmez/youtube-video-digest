Essentials: Compulsive Behaviors & Deep Brain Stimulation | Dr. Casey Halpern

Dr. Casey Halpern, a stereotactic functional neurosurgeon, discusses how DBS and focused ultrasound are being used to treat conditions beyond movement disorders, specifically targeting psychiatric comorbidities like depression and obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD). He explains that stimulating brain circuits involved in emotion can yield therapeutic effects even when treating motor symptoms. OCD is viewed as a spectrum disorder involving dysregulation in cortical areas like the prefrontal cortex and subcortical regions such as the ventral striatum, which gates reward-seeking behavior. While medications and Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) are standard treatments, DBS offers hope for severe, treatment-resistant cases by modulating these specific brain circuits. The field is moving toward non-invasive methods to improve precision in targeting these compulsive behaviors.
