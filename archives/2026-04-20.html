How to Better Regulate Your Emotions | Dr. Marc Brackett

Emotion regulation is not about suppressing or getting rid of feelings; instead, it involves developing another relationship with those emotions, such as simply acknowledging them. The speaker cautions against the misconception that one must constantly monitor their feelings, noting that most emotions operate in the background unless a significant environmental shift occurs. Effective regulation requires understanding how individual context and emotion function influence strategy selection—for instance, dealing with anxiety differs from handling anger. Furthermore, societal pressures often dictate what emotions are deemed acceptable for certain groups, such as discouraging boys from expressing vulnerable feelings like sadness or disappointment. The core message is to move from automatic, habitual reactions to deliberate, conscious responses by creating space between a stimulus and a reaction.
