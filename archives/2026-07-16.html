This video provides an educational overview of the science, types, and treatments for Bipolar Disorder.

Andrew Huberman · 2026-07-16T12:00:15+00:00

The Science & Treatment of Bipolar Disorder | Huberman Lab Essentials

Bipolar Disorder involves massive shifts in mood, energy, and perception, posing a significant risk, with sufferers being 20 to 30 times more likely to attempt suicide. The condition is categorized as Bipolar One (characterized by manic episodes lasting seven days or more) and Bipolar Two (often involving hypomanic episodes lasting four days or less alongside depressive episodes). Mania involves symptoms like distractibility, grandiosity, flight of ideas, agitation, and decreased need for sleep. Treatment discussions cover the historical discovery of lithium's efficacy in reducing mania, which is thought to protect against neurotoxicity associated with overactive neural circuits. While drug therapy combined with talk therapies like Cognitive Behavioral Therapy is recommended, lifestyle changes and supplements such as omega-3 fatty acids can offer supportive care.

  • Bipolar One requires manic episodes lasting seven days or more.
  • Bipolar Two involves hypomanic episodes and depressive episodes.
  • Lithium has shown profound effects in reducing mania by potentially preventing neurotoxicity.
  • Drug therapy combined with talk therapies is most effective for Bipolar Disorder.
  • Lifestyle interventions like sleep, exercise, and omega-3s can support the nervous system.
