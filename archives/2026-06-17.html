Can you cheat cloud insurance?

This short clip explains cloud insurance by analogy to ordinary insurance. The guest says customers like having an exit from unused cloud commitments because they can recover dollars and reinvest them instead of resenting a cloud provider. That can benefit the ecosystem in the same way car insurance helps people feel comfortable buying an expensive vehicle. Scott then asks the abuse question: could someone buy or backdate insurance when they already know a startup is failing? The answer is that this is the classic insurance problem of underwriting and moral hazard: the insurer has to avoid selling coverage in a way that encourages risk-taking or lets a buyer shift a known loss onto the insurer.

  • Cloud insurance is framed as a way to turn unused cloud commitments into usable budget instead of sunk frustration.
  • The ecosystem analogy is car insurance: risk transfer can make large purchases easier to make.
  • The obvious attack is buying coverage after the risk is already known or using insurance to take more risk.
  • The control mechanism is underwriting, specifically screening and pricing to avoid moral hazard.
