What 20 Squats a Day Actually Does to Your Body (9 Benefits Explained)

Performing just twenty bodyweight squats each day can yield significant physiological changes, affecting everything from muscle mass to brain health. These simple movements stimulate the mTOR switch in large leg and glute muscles, helping combat sarcopenia, which is linked to increased fracture risk. Furthermore, squatting boosts mood by releasing endorphins and BDNF, supporting neuroplasticity and potentially reducing dementia risks. The exercise also provides a workout for the heart and aids circulation by engaging the 'second heart' in the legs. Additionally, squats improve insulin sensitivity by allowing muscles to absorb glucose without needing insulin, strengthen bones through mechanical loading, enhance balance, and increase overall mobility. These benefits accumulate over time, contributing to better aging.
