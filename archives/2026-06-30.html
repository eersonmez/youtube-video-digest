Grant Sanderson (3Blue1Brown) – AI and the future of math

The discussion with Grant Sanderson from 3Blue1Brown explores how Artificial Intelligence is rapidly advancing in mathematics, noting that its progress may not manifest as simple benchmarks. He suggests that AI excels at connecting disparate fields, similar to how solutions to complex problems like Fermat's Last Theorem require bridging different mathematical areas. Instead of headline breakthroughs, the impact might be a subtle shift in mathematicians' conversations, where AI aids in conceptual framing and research direction. While AIs could automate theorem proving, the creative leap—the initial insight or conjecture—remains challenging for current models. The future role of humans may evolve toward curation, much like art museum curators navigating vast landscapes of ideas generated by AI.
