The medieval reverence for Emperor Trajan, despite his pagan beliefs.

Dwarkesh Patel · 2026-06-20T20:22:07+00:00

They loved Trajan so much they rewrote the afterlife for him - Ada Palmer

Medieval Europe struggled with reconciling the image of good emperors like Trajan with the concept that they might be in hell due to their paganism. This led to a popular legend where Pope Gregory the Great supposedly summoned and baptized Trajan's ghost so he could attain heaven. Despite being an emperor during Christian persecution, his stature as a great Caesar was so immense that medieval chroniclers insisted on his posthumous baptism. Dante further cemented this view by placing him in Paradiso as the ideal Christian ruler. This reflects how Medieval and Renaissance Europe selectively adopted elements from both pagan antiquity and Christianity to construct their idealized versions of the past.

  • Medieval belief required good emperors to be saved from hell.
  • Pope Gregory the Great is credited with baptizing Trajan's ghost.
  • Trajan was a persecutor of Christians but revered as a great Caesar.
  • Dante depicted Trajan in Paradiso as an ideal ruler.
  • European antiquity celebrated both pagan and Christian virtues.
