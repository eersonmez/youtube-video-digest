## Workspace agents in ChatGPT: Admin and builder controls

- Channel: OpenAI
- Published: 2026-05-22T22:48:37+00:00
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HaaKUFAOi84

This video showcases ChatGPT's Workspace agents for Business, Enterprise, and Edu tiers, focusing on the configuration tools available to builders for setting up automations and constraints, as well as the governance features for administrators to secure and monitor agent deployments across their organization.

Key points:
- Workspace agents enable persistent, team-scoped automation across integrations like Google Drive, Gmail, Slack, and Linear.
- Builder controls allow configuration of specific read/write permissions for tools and execution of scheduled background tasks.
- Builders can enforce natural language constraints (e.g., restricting emails to specific domains) and implement human-in-the-loop validation for sensitive actions.
- Admin controls provide centralized governance, letting IT admins manage agent publishing and enforce workspace-wide policies.
- Admins can override builder settings to globally block write actions to particular apps or restrict app usage by role.
- Compliance features include a dedicated API and monitoring tools to maintain auditing and enterprise-grade security.

## Essentials: The Science of Learning & Speaking Languages | Dr. Eddie Chang

- Channel: Andrew Huberman
- Published: 2026-05-21T12:00:25+00:00
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MOPqSEK6GvA

In this episode, Dr. Andrew Huberman hosts Dr. Eddie Chang, a neurosurgeon and professor at UCSF who specializes in the neurobiology of speech and language. They discuss the critical differences between speech (the motor output) and language (cognitive understanding), how early exposure to sounds shapes the infant auditory cortex, and the potential developmental issues associated with continuous white noise. They also cover the lateralization of language processing in the brain, clinical insights from awake brain surgery, and Dr. Chang's pioneering work with brain-machine interfaces (BMIs) that translate neural activity into text, speech, and expressive avatars to restore communication to paralyzed individuals.

Key points:
- Speech vs. Language: Speech is a complex motor process involving precise control of the vocal tract, while language is the cognitive system of semantics, grammar, and meaning.
- Auditory Development and White Noise: During early critical development windows, the brain adapts to environmental sounds. Continuous unstructured white noise can delay auditory cortex maturation, so natural sounds are recommended instead.
- Bilingualism and the Brain: Learning multiple languages early leads to overlapping neural circuits in the brain, with distinct temporal processing for each language.
- Awake Brain Surgery: Performing mapping while patients are conscious allows surgeons to safely navigate and remove tumors or treat epilepsy without damaging critical language zones.
- Brain-Machine Interfaces (BMIs): Electrode arrays combined with AI decoders can translate attempts to speak directly into text or synthesized voice, helping patients with locked-in syndrome or paralysis regain communication.
- Avatar Integration: Modern BCI technology has progressed to control digital avatars that express facial movements and tone, enabling more natural and holistic communication.

## How I use LLMs

- Channel: Andrej Karpathy
- Published: 2025-02-27T22:29:56+00:00
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EWvNQjAaOHw

In this video, Andrej Karpathy provides a comprehensive, hands-on guide showing how he integrates LLMs into his daily workflow and software development projects. Rather than treating LLMs as standalone agents, he positions them as powerful, collaborative tools. He details key practical guidelines such as organizing knowledge into markdown files, querying multiple models to compare outputs, utilizing voice dictation, keeping chat contexts fresh, and using thinking models for difficult tasks. He also highlights the importance of tools like Python interpreters and multimodal inputs.

Key points:
- View LLMs as collaborative, over-confident assistants (like a pair programmer) that excel at generating ideas and boilerplate, but require strict human verification and quality control.
- Utilize an 'LLM Council' approach by comparing responses from multiple leading models (e.g., ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Grok) to obtain a broader perspective or choose the best output.
- Adopt 'thinking' or reasoning-centric models for complex tasks like math, logic, or heavy debugging, where multi-step internal pondering yields superior results compared to standard models.
- Leverage voice as a primary interface (roughly 80% of personal usage) to increase speed and reduce friction in brainstorming and dialog.
- Maintain clean context windows by starting fresh chats when switching topics to prevent confusion, model hallucination, and performance degradation.
- Build a structured personal knowledge base ('LLM Wiki') using markdown files, grounding the LLMs on personal data to minimize general internet hallucinations.
- Embrace multimodality and tool integration, including using Python interpreters for mathematical verification or plotting, web search for up-to-date information, and image/video uploads for visual analysis.
