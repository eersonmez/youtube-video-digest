Metabolic Warning Sign Hiding in a Gout test (Uric Acid)

The video discusses how uric acid, often only associated with gout, is actually one of the earliest indicators that a person's metabolism is deteriorating, potentially years before blood sugar abnormalities appear. While standard lab ranges (below 6.8 or 7 mg/dL) are for gout, optimal levels for cardiometabolic health and insulin resistance should be below 5 mg/dL, with women possibly needing levels under 4 mg/dL. Catching this early is crucial because metabolic issues are easier to reverse before permanent damage occurs from conditions like high blood pressure or heart disease.

  • Uric acid can predict diabetes and insulin resistance years in advance.

  • Fructose and added sugars are major drivers of elevated uric acid.
