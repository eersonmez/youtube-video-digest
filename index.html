Andrew Huberman · 2026-07-09T12:00:07+00:00

The Science & Treatment of Obsessive Compulsive Disorder (OCD) | Huberman Lab Essentials

Obsessive Compulsive Disorder (OCD) is a common yet debilitating condition characterized by intrusive obsessions and compulsions that often reinforce each other, leading to significant functional impairment. The underlying mechanism involves a dysfunctional loop within the cortical-striatal-thalamic circuit of the brain. While genetics play a role in about 40 to 50 percent of cases, effective treatments target this neural circuitry. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), particularly exposure therapy, has shown dramatic efficacy by teaching anxiety tolerance without engaging in compulsions. When comparing treatments, CBT alone was more effective than SSRIs or placebo, and combining them did not yield further benefit. Other interventions like Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS) show promise in interrupting compulsive behaviors, while mindfulness meditation can aid focus for therapy.

  • OCD involves intrusive obsessions and compulsions that strengthen the obsession.
  • The cortical-striatal-thalamic loop is believed to underlie OCD pathology.
  • Exposure-based Cognitive Behavioral Therapy is highly effective in reducing symptoms.
  • CBT alone demonstrated greater symptom reduction than SSRIs or placebo.
  • TMS shows potential for interrupting automatic compulsive behaviors.

  • It versions the activity between commits, including edits, prompts, and agent conversations.
  • Work trees and agent sessions become collaborative artifacts that teammates can join later.
  • Fine-grained deltas allow code to be traced across time, movement, deletion, and refactoring.
  • Sobo acknowledges privacy risks and discusses controls for sharing only selected history.
