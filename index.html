  • Template metaprogramming redirects compiler behavior, but its complexity and eager conditional evaluation can cause recursion and instantiation-depth problems.
  • constexpr programming uses standard C++ code at compile time, with distinct meta-runtime and meta-compile-time scopes and limitations around arguments.
  • The type system uses continuation-passing-style composition, serialized flat-array records, and stack-allocated memory.

A deep but accessible introduction to general relativity – Adam Brown

The core concept is summarized by the slogan: matter tells spacetime how to curve, and curved spacetime tells matter how to move along straight lines within that curvature. Key concepts include the equivalence principle—the observation that inertial mass equals gravitational mass—and the prediction that light bends twice as much in GR compared to Newtonian physics. Furthermore, the theory predicts phenomena like time dilation near massive objects and provides a framework for understanding black holes, such as the event horizon.

  • General Relativity describes gravity not as a force but as spacetime curvature caused by mass and energy.
  • The equivalence principle suggests that inertial mass is equivalent to gravitational mass.
  • GR predicts phenomena like gravitational time dilation near massive objects.
  • Black holes are predicted regions where the gravitational field becomes infinite at the event horizon.
  • Observational evidence, such as the precession of stars around Sagittarius A*, supports GR.
