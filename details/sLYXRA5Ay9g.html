Sephora is leveraging AI across its operations to enhance customer experience and internal efficiency.

What This Video Is About

This video details how Sephora is strategically integrating Artificial Intelligence across its extensive global operations to enhance customer experience, drive internal efficiency, and deepen brand engagement. With 80 million active loyalty members and over 2.5 billion annual visits across its digital platforms, Sephora views AI as central to delivering a superior omnichannel experience. The implementation spans from optimizing website personalization and generating marketing content using generative AI, to improving supply chain forecasting and media investment through advanced analytics.

The core focus of the presentation is on two specific customer-facing initiatives developed in collaboration with OpenAI: a "Shopping Assistant" designed to digitally replicate the personalized advice provided by in-store Beauty Advisors online, and the integration of the Sephora app directly into ChatGPT. These projects aim to bridge the gap between physical store expertise and digital accessibility, leading to measurable business impacts such as increased conversion rates.

Detailed Walkthrough

Sephora leverages AI across its entire value chain [0:44]. Digitally, AI optimizes websites by providing tailored product recommendations, next best content, and relevant promotions based on customer responses—for instance, tailoring offers between discounts or gifts [1:26]. Generative AI is utilized to assist marketing teams in creating social media assets, advertising campaigns, and internal communications [1:48]. Operationally, supply chain teams use AI for improved sales forecasting to dictate inventory orders from brand partners [2:12], while media teams employ marketing mix models to optimize investment across different channels by understanding the impact of spend adjustments [2:12].

Focusing on customer experience, Sephora developed two key initiatives with OpenAI. The first is the Shopping Assistant, which aims to recreate the personalized advice given by in-store Beauty Advisors—who typically recommend products based on skin type and tone and explain application methods [3:01]. This assistant enables natural conversations online, offering tailored product suggestions and beauty advice for individuals [3:25]. A demonstration shows a user searching for "mascara" and then using the AI Beauty feature to ask specific questions about ingredients or whether the mascara is waterproof [4:37]. The customer can then add the item directly to their basket and proceed to checkout, demonstrating its role in driving conversion [5:00].

The second initiative involves integrating the Sephora app directly into ChatGPT, allowing engagement through a platform customers already use daily [3:25].

From building the AI Beauty Assistant, several key lessons were derived. First, guided experiences perform better; instead of expecting users to type complex queries, providing suggested prompts like "Gifts under €50" increases interaction rates because users are naturally less inclined to type extensive input [5:33, 6:11]. Second, the user experience must be seamless and maintain Sephora's specific tone of voice so customers feel they are receiving advice from a Beauty Advisor rather than just an AI [6:26]. Third, transparency is crucial; when recommending a product, the chatbot should explain the reasoning—such as basing the suggestion on purchase history or brand preferences [6:53]. Finally, Sephora maintains a test-and-learn mindset, running numerous A/B tests to continuously refine and improve the assistant's performance [7:15].

Key Takeaways

  • AI supports personalization across websites and apps by tailoring content and promotions.
  • The Shopping Assistant recreates in-store beauty advice online through natural conversation capabilities.
  • Guided prompts, such as "Gifts under €50," significantly improve chatbot interaction rates compared to open-ended queries.
  • Maintaining Sephora's specific tone of voice is essential for the AI assistant to feel like a trusted Beauty Advisor.
  • Transparency regarding product recommendations—explaining why a product was suggested (e.g., based on purchase history)—is a critical design principle.
  • The integration of the Sephora app into ChatGPT expands customer touchpoints across familiar platforms.
  • A test-and-learn methodology, involving numerous A/B tests, is necessary for continuous enhancement of AI experiences.

Practical Notes

  • Shopping Assistant Functionality: Allows users to ask specific questions about products (e.g., "What ingredients are in this mascara?") and receive immediate answers while remaining on the product page [4:37].
  • Business Impact Metric: The Shopping Assistant has increased conversion rates by more than 5%, which is noted as a significant uplift compared to typical personalization initiatives yielding around 1% [5:17].
  • Design Principle (Guidance): Implement suggested prompts rather than relying solely on free-form user input to boost engagement.
  • Design Principle (Tone/UX): Ensure the AI assistant reflects the established brand tone of voice to maintain customer trust and perceived quality of advice [6:26].
