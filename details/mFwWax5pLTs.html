Verso is an AI-native company that operates with near-full autonomy by utilizing its 'Company Brain'.

OpenAI · 2026-06-26T11:00:22+00:00 · Verso, l'entreprise qui ne dort jamais

What This Video Is About

This video details how Verso, an AI-native company, has achieved near-full operational autonomy since its inception by implementing a system called the "Verso Brain." The core mission of Verso is to act as a context provider for clients, delivering real-time consumer insights that enable businesses to make high-impact financial decisions. By automating complex processes like end-to-end consumer research, Verso drastically reduces the time and cost associated with traditional market research methods.

The company leverages advanced AI capabilities, powered by OpenAI models, to orchestrate everything from study design and respondent recruitment to AI-moderated interviews and final data analysis. This level of automation allows a very small team to manage significant business volume, demonstrating how an organization can be built around autonomous agents rather than traditional human workflows.

Detailed Walkthrough

Lydia, the co-founder and CEO of Verso, introduces the company's premise: they are context providers who give clients real-time access to consumer thoughts, decision-making processes, and motivations [0:43]. They have built an agentic solution that manages the entire consumer research lifecycle—from designing a study to recruiting participants, using an AI interviewer for moderation, and delivering actionable insights [1:06]. This process allows clients to receive consumer insights ten times faster and at half the cost of traditional methods [1:41].

The product is powered end-to-end by OpenAI. For the interview stage, they utilize Voice models and the Realtime Translation model [1:59]. The analysis phase relies on a sophisticated multi-agent pipeline built upon the latest frontier models [2:09].

The central focus of the presentation shifts to how Verso operates as an autonomous organization through the "Verso Brain" [2:42]. This brain functions based on three core capabilities: Listen, Think, and Act.

  • Listen: The Company Brain gathers data from both internal sources (emails, calendars, Slack, Notion) and external sources, with this orchestration managed via Codex automations [2:57].
  • Think: This component is composed of two parts: the intelligence layer, which is the Knowledge Agent powered by Cortex; it indexes incoming information to retrieve relevant context for specific use cases [3:20]. It also incorporates memory, a flexible database managed by the Knowledge Agent that stores all organizational context and Verso's internal information [3:32].
  • Act: This is the execution layer, allowing the Brain to take real-world action through tools, MCP (Model Control Protocols), and CLIs, executing workflows autonomously [3:45].

To illustrate this, Lydia details how the Verso Brain manages a consumer research study workflow. Traditionally, delivering such a study takes about one week for scoping, three weeks for fieldwork, and another three weeks for analysis—a slow, expensive process requiring many Customer Success Managers (CSMs) [4:06]. At Verso, the Brain interacts directly with client knowledge and the Verso application because the product was designed from day one to be controllable by AI agents.

A concrete example is provided regarding a Slack interaction [5:16]. A team member tagged the Verso Brain in a client's Slack channel after a meeting, asking it to retrieve information from that meeting and related email exchanges. An hour and a half later, fieldwork was already underway [6:23]. Over the weekend, fifty consumers in the US completed the study via an AI interviewer; by Sunday afternoon, fieldwork was complete. Upon arriving at the office Monday morning, the Verso Brain had flagged interviews for review due to potential fraud. After validation, analysis launched, and the study was delivered within two hours [5:58]. This contrasts sharply with the traditional six-week wait time, achieving insights in just seventy-two hours.

Furthermore, this autonomy extends to engineering operations. At most tech companies, engineers spend about twenty percent of their time fixing bugs; at Verso, AI agents automatically detect and triage every bug, fixing ninety percent of them without human intervention [7:08]. This allows the small team to focus almost entirely on product development and value creation.
