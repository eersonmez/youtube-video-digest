Advent leverages ChatGPT and Codex to accelerate AI adoption within its portfolio companies.

OpenAI · 2026-06-29T14:30:09+00:00 · Advent Accelerates Deals with ChatGPT + Codex

What This Video Is About

This video discusses how Advent is strategically accelerating the adoption of Artificial Intelligence within its portfolio companies by addressing the common hurdle where organizations have high aspirations for AI but lack clear starting points or implementation strategies. The core strategy involves leveraging advanced tools like custom GPT instances and Codex to provide immediate, practical value to these businesses.

The approach focuses on grounding AI applications in real-world company data, allowing teams to move beyond theoretical discussions into actionable workflows. By connecting internal knowledge bases—such as emails and Slack conversations—to generative AI models, Advent enables real-time querying capabilities that significantly enhance operational efficiency for the companies they support.

Detailed Walkthrough

Advent observes that many organizations express significant interest in Artificial Intelligence but struggle with knowing where to begin implementation [0:00]. To tackle this, one of the most natural use cases being employed is the development of custom GPT instances tailored specifically for each portfolio company [0:09]. These custom GPT setups are designed to ingest and connect a deal folder with vast amounts of internal context that resides across various sources, including email inboxes and Slack channels [0:17-0:25]. This integration allows teams the ability to ask real-time questions about this connected context, which has proven highly impactful on their workflows [0:33].

Furthermore, Advent's portfolio support group utilizes Codex as a mechanism to rapidly create Minimum Viable Products (MVPs) that can address genuine business challenges faced by these companies [0:36]. A concrete example provided is the creation of a contract management system. This system was effectively "vibe coded" using these methods before it was considered for further development into a fully realized, commercial product [0:45-0:53].
