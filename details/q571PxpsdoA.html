What This Video Is About

This video is about how Three Wishes, a family-run “better for you” breakfast cereal company, uses GPT-5.6 and Codex to build an internal operational tool for managing seasonal product launches. The brand describes its cereal as high in protein, lower in sugar, gluten-free, and grain-free, with the original inspiration coming from the founders’ own home and family [0:12].

The practical problem is growth. As Three Wishes becomes larger, the founders say mistakes carry more weight, and seasonal launches have become an “operational nightmare” [0:33]-[0:44]. A seasonal product such as a pumpkin spice launch involves retailer-specific deadlines, contacts, timelines, and sell-in tracking. Those moving pieces are difficult for a small team to coordinate manually.

The video positions Codex as a substitute for the technical co-founder the business previously felt it lacked. The founders say they had many technology ideas in the past but did not feel able to execute them because they needed technical help [0:38]. With Codex and GPT-5.6, they describe converting a messy spoken thought process into a branded command center and dashboard informed by historical launch data and company brand guidelines.

The core importance is not cereal itself, but the shift in what a small business can build internally. The founders frame the resulting tool as “bespoke software” that previously felt accessible only to much larger companies [1:30]. For them, the value is operational control and time saved: time that can go back into growing the brand or spending time with their children [1:42].
