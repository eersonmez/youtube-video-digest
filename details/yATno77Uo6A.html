What This Video Is About

This video details how the director of product marketing at Stampliy is leveraging AI-centric approaches, specifically utilizing GPT-powered systems, to fundamentally transform their product marketing workflow. The central problem addressed is the historically manual and labor-intensive process of keeping all product assets current. Previously, this required significant effort involving interviews with product managers, manually sifting through documentation in Jira and GitHub [0:24-0:31].

The solution implemented involves an automated system designed to extract necessary information from various sources. This automation has drastically increased efficiency, enabling a single person to manage the workload that previously required a team of four or five individuals, resulting in the production of hundreds of content pieces weekly [0:41-0:47]. The shift allows marketing staff to transition their focus from tedious manual processing and writing toward high-level strategic advising.

Detailed Walkthrough

The workflow at Stampliy has evolved from a purely manual process to one heavily augmented by AI. Historically, updating product assets involved several manual steps: interviewing product managers for information, reading through Jira tickets, and reviewing GitHub repositories to extract relevant data [0:24-0:31].

The current setup utilizes a GPT-powered system that automates the extraction of necessary information from different sources. Specifically, this system is capable of extracting data directly from Jira [0:33] and transcribing meeting notes while simultaneously extracting context from those transcripts [0:37-0:39]. During live meetings, an individual has Codex connected to multiple systems via API. This connection allows the user to extract all required data and reporting in real time, providing answers directly during the call [0:51-0:57]. This capability frees up staff time, allowing them to operate as strategic advisors rather than being bogged down by manual processing tasks [1:02-1:07].
