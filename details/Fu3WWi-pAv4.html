Emmanuel Marill discusses building AI-native companies by prioritizing AI from the start.

OpenAI · 2026-07-16T15:37:30+00:00 · Think AI-First: Emmanuel Marill on Building AI-Native Companies | OpenAI France

What This Video Is About

The central theme of this video is the necessity of adopting an "AI-first" mindset when building new companies or products, rather than merely attempting to integrate artificial intelligence into existing systems. The speaker argues against the common pitfall of adding AI features to a pre-existing product with the hope that it will function well. Instead, the core strategy involves first determining what capabilities AI can offer initially and then constructing the entire business model around those inherent strengths [0:16].

This approach is exemplified by companies like Verso, which built their entire operational framework around an AI component referred to as a "brain," making them inherently AI-native and highly efficient. The advice extends across various organizational scales, from startups to large global corporations, emphasizing that this methodology is applicable throughout the entire technological ecosystem [0:54].

Detailed Walkthrough

The speaker advocates for a disciplined approach to product development centered on AI capability. The fundamental shift required is moving away from trying to "put some AI in something that is not working" [0:08] toward asking, "what does AI can do initially and then I can build on that" [0:16].

A key component of this strategy is being laser focused. One must identify a very specific set of problems—perhaps only two or three—that the company intends to solve [0:33]. Once these critical problems are identified, the process requires going "very deep step by step into the workflow" associated with solving them [0:40]. At every single stage of that workflow, the objective is to analyze and determine precisely how AI can contribute to improving that specific step [0:40].

The speaker notes that this focus must be on fixing "real problems for your company," rather than tackling broad or generalized issues [0:47]. The strength of the current ecosystem in research and startups is acknowledged, but the advice remains consistent: build around what AI can do from the ground up.

Key Takeaways

  • Adopt an AI-first mindset instead of retrofitting AI onto established products.
  • Determine the initial capabilities of AI before designing or building any surrounding product structure.
  • Maintain extreme focus by selecting only a few specific, real problems to solve for the company.
  • Conduct a granular, step-by-step analysis of existing workflows.
  • At every stage of the workflow, evaluate precisely how AI can drive improvement in that process.
  • The principle applies equally well to small startups and very large global corporations.

Practical Notes

No specific commands, product names (other than Verso), or detailed architectural blueprints were provided beyond the conceptual framework described. The decision point for any organization is whether they are attempting to bolt AI onto an existing solution or if they are defining their core value proposition based on what AI can achieve initially.
