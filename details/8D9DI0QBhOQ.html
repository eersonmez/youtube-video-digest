What This Video Is About

This video discusses a limitation in current version control practices concerning AI-assisted software development. Standard systems like Git are effective at versioning final checkpoints or artifacts—the completed pieces of code or files that are committed. However, the discussion centers on the concept of "dark matter," which refers to all the intermediate activity occurring between these official commits. This includes abandoned edits, specific prompts given to AI agents, and the various steering decisions made during the development process.

The core idea presented is that version control needs to evolve beyond just tracking final snapshots. The goal is to capture and make available this entire developmental journey across any range of time scales, including real-time tracking. Currently, the typical workflow assumes a one-on-one interaction between a developer and an AI agent, leading only to discrete snapshots being pushed to platforms like GitHub after the conversation concludes.
