A family uses GPT-5.6 to manage and scale their small cereal business.

OpenAI · 2026-07-09T20:00:18+00:00 · Meet a family running a cereal business from their dining room with GPT-5.6.

What This Video Is About

This video details how the creators of Three Wishes, a healthy breakfast cereal, leveraged OpenAI's GPT-5.6 to manage and scale their small business operations as the brand grew. The central problem addressed is that seasonal launches had become an "operational nightmare" for the family team. They utilized GPT-5.6 not just as a general assistant, but as a technical co-founder capable of building complex operational tools.

The core value proposition demonstrated is the ability to generate highly detailed, bespoke enterprise software and dashboards using natural language prompts. By feeding the AI specific historical data, new branding guidelines (such as new box designs), and clear goals, the family achieved outputs that previously would have required significant resources from a large corporation. This capability allowed them to shift their focus from complex logistics to brand building and personal time.

Detailed Walkthrough

The process began with identifying operational bottlenecks during seasonal launches [0:44]. The team decided they needed a "technical co-founder," which they implemented using Codex (GPT-5.6). A key initial request was to build a sell and timeline tracker that incorporated retailer-specific deadlines and contacts for a specific launch, such as the pumpkin spice flavor [0:01].

The workflow involved providing GPT-5.6 with comprehensive instructions. The user emphasized setting an end-to-end goal and using historical launches as data sources to inform the resulting dashboard [0:52]. Crucially, they directed the AI to adhere strictly to Three Wishes' branding, specifically mentioning new box designs and brand guidelines [1:01], instructing it to "make it look like and feel like our brand" [1:12].

The result was an output described as having "incredibly amazing output," which felt like bespoke enterprise software rather than a simple AI response [1:05, 1:39]. The speaker noted that GPT-5.6's capability in handling these nuanced, branded outputs represented a substantial leap over previous models like 5.5, allowing the family to generate complex data slices with minimal input time—only about five minutes of prompting yielded the necessary results [1:21]. This system effectively provided them with an operational command center and dashboard for managing seasonal launches [0:44].
