What This Video Is About

This video details the personal routine adopted by Steve Hasker, the CEO of Thomson Reuters, as a framework for navigating the company's broad transformation involving Artificial Intelligence (AI) adoption. The central theme emphasized throughout his approach is the necessity of authenticity in driving such large-scale organizational change.

Hasker outlines specific daily and weekly habits designed to ensure he remains grounded in the practical applications and real-world implications of new AI tools within the business context. His methodology combines personal hands-on usage with continuous external learning from industry peers, competitors, customers, and partners. This routine is presented as a model for how leadership can actively engage with emerging technologies while fostering an environment of shared learning across the organization.

Detailed Walkthrough

The discussion begins by establishing that the entire company is engaged in a collective journey of learning regarding AI [0:00]. Hasker posits that authenticity is the essential component required for successfully managing a broad transformation like the current AI rollout at Thomson Reuters [0:09]. His personal routine starts on Monday mornings with direct engagement with the available tools. This usage can involve analyzing documents pertaining to the day or week, or reviewing his calendar to extract insights and prepare for the coming week [0:17-0:26].

A significant part of his strategy involves monitoring who is moving quickly—identifying early movers, fastest movers, and those making mistakes that lead to valuable learnings [0:30-0:37]. To accelerate the company's learning journey, he maintains constant dialogue with his team, specifically asking what observations they are gathering from external sources such as peers, competitors, customers, and partners [0:37-0:44].

For global context, Hasker incorporates listening to the Reuters News World podcast. Since it is recorded predominantly in the UK, this provides a ready overview of breaking news, geopolitics, and conflicts upon starting his Monday morning routine [0:48-0:56]. His overarching advice for others involves getting personally immersed in using the tools themselves. Furthermore, he stresses the importance of maintaining continuous dialogue with teams concerning both the benefits derived from these new tools and any pitfalls or learnings encountered during implementation [1:04].
