What This Video Is About

This video provides a curated review of various innovative and practical smart home devices and tools that the reviewer deems worthwhile for modern households. The content spans different categories, including aesthetic displays like e-ink dashboards, specialized automation hardware such as garage door openers and sensors, maintenance gadgets, cleaning robotics, and advanced construction aids.

The central theme is showcasing technology that moves beyond simple novelty to offer genuine utility in managing a smart home or performing household tasks more efficiently. The reviewer emphasizes selecting items based on actual usefulness rather than just hype, often comparing new devices against existing market offerings or personal past experiences.

Detailed Walkthrough

The video begins by introducing the Terminal OG, an e-ink smart home dashboard [0:27]. This device allows users to display custom dashboards created in Home Assistant—showing information like weather stations, door locks, batteries, and calendars. The Terminal app takes a snapshot of this customized Home Assistant view, applies styling for the e-ink display, and sends it to refresh the screen. Because it uses very little battery power, it can operate for weeks or months without recharging [0:34].

Next, the reviewer presents the Third Reality Garage Door Opener [1:19], a simple yet effective robot button designed to interface with garage door remotes, bypassing limitations imposed by manufacturers like Evil Corp. The setup involves sticking a tilt sensor on the door to track its open or closed status and using rechargeable batteries within the device itself [1:34]. This opener is compatible with Matter systems [1:50].

A small gadget reviewed is a battery tester [2:04] that can quickly check battery levels for AA/AAA (1.5 volts), rechargeable (1.2 volts), and coin cells (3 volts). It uses the battery being tested to power its display, meaning it won't show anything if the battery is dead [2:16]. Another inexpensive add-on mentioned allows users to monitor charging status of smart home devices by displaying various technical statistics [2:40].

The discussion moves to smart buttons. For Amazon Echo users, a specific button is highlighted that supports both short and long presses, allowing it to execute up to eight different Amazon routines from one physical device [3:10]. Alternatively, for Matter users with Thread border routers, IKEA offers inexpensive smart buttons (around 6) that can use rechargeable AAA batteries and attach magnetically. A more advanced option is an IKEA button featuring a scroll wheel (10), which can adjust light brightness while also controlling three different groups of lights [3:40].

For battery-free control, the Zeus Zen32 is featured as a favorite device [3:58]. It has a main on/off switch and dimmer versions. The reviewer uses the on/off version for fans up to 3 amps, while the bottom buttons can be programmed to control other smart devices in the room; LED colors can indicate what each button controls, making it family-friendly [4:07]. This device requires a Z-Wave hub [4:47].

In terms of home maintenance tools, the Ark Pro and Arc Pro Ultra flashlights are reviewed for their brightness relative to size, suitability for clipping onto hats, and multiple light settings including wide field of view, spotlight, laser pointer, and black light (useful for scorpions in Arizona) [5:02].

The reviewer then examines an IKEA air quality sensor [5:43] that costs $30. It monitors temperature, humidity, 2.5 particulate matter, and CO2 levels. While it resets settings when unplugged and its sensors are not as precise as premium models, its low cost and Matter compatibility make it acceptable for the price point [6:09].

The review transitions to cleaning robotics sponsored by Robbo Rock. The Kreo S Pro is noted for features like a self-cleaning dock and anti-tangle side brush, suitable for pet owners [6:32]. For mopping, the Curve Two Flow is highlighted for its roller mop design, featuring a scraper to remove gunk and an automatic shield that covers the roller when transitioning to carpet [7:04]. A more comprehensive option is the F-25 Ultra, which functions as both a wet and dry cleaner, capable of steaming or using hot water for stain removal, with a feature to clean and dry its roller after deep cleaning [7:23].

The Accara U400 smart door lock with ultra wideband is presented [7:57]. Its key feature is the ability for the door to automatically unlock simply by walking up to it using Ultra Wideband technology, which works on compatible phones (like newer iPhones) [8:05]. It also includes a fingerprint reader and uses Matter over Thread.

Smart lighting solutions include a star projector that creates an outer space experience on the ceiling with various color themes and laser stars [8:58]. Another item is a floor lamp that offers high brightness, warm/cool white settings, multiple colors simultaneously, and can be controlled by hand waving [9:28]. Both of these items work with Matter for automated scheduling.

A highly praised tool is the Fantic S2 Pro powered screwdriver [10:07]. It comes in a compact case holding magnetic bits, includes a right-angle adapter, allows users to adjust light bar strength, and features a lock mechanism enabling manual tightening or loosening of screws. The reviewer states it has replaced their traditional drill and screwdriver for nearly all installation tasks [10:48].

For automation sensors, the Third Reality R3 millimeter wave sensor is detailed [11:11]. It includes an RGB light, a light sensor, and a VOCC air quality sensor. Its responsiveness allows it to keep lights on as long as someone remains in the area (e.g., garage lighting). It uses Zigbee and acts as a repeater for nearby devices. The reviewer suggests using this bulky R3 in garages while preferring the smaller Accara FP1E inside the house [12:01].

In terms of construction aids, the Sapphire 9000 stud finder is lauded for its extreme accuracy, showing exactly where studs are located via lights [12:57]. Complementary to this is a camera probe, which features an end light and joystick allowing users to view tight spaces without needing a phone camera [13:12].

Finally, the video showcases two entertainment/security gadgets. A chess robot can set up the board and move pieces; it requires an overhead light for accurate board reading, and its processing is done locally via a camera focused only on the chessboard [14:03]. Lastly, the TP-Link TapO camera is reviewed for its 24/7 recording capability powered by solar energy. While most solar cameras rely on event recording, this model takes snapshots at default 10-second intervals between motion events, which are then presented as a time-lapse [14:57].

Key Takeaways

  • The Terminal OG is an e-ink dashboard that displays custom Home Assistant dashboards by receiving snapshot images from the terminal app.
  • The Third Reality Garage Door Opener functions as a robot button to press garage door remotes and integrates with Matter.
  • A specialized battery tester can check voltage for AA/AAA, rechargeable (1.2V), and coin cells (3V) batteries.
  • Smart buttons are available compatible with Amazon Echo or IKEA's Matter ecosystem, offering customizable short/long press routines.
  • The Zeus Zen32 is a Z-Wave device that can control fans (up to 3 amps) and other smart devices via programmable buttons.
  • IKEA offers an affordable air quality sensor monitoring temperature, humidity, particulate matter, and CO2, compatible with Matter.
  • Robbo Rock offers vacuum models like the Kreo S Pro with anti-tangle brushes, and mopping options like the Curve Two Flow with a scraper/shield system.
  • The Accara U400 smart lock utilizes Ultra Wideband technology for automatic unlocking when approached by a compatible phone.
  • The Fantic S2 Pro powered screwdriver is a versatile tool that includes magnetic bit storage, an adjustable light bar, and a locking mechanism for manual screw manipulation.
  • Millimeter wave sensors like the Third Reality R3 can be used in areas like garages to trigger automations based on presence while providing extra sensor data (RGB/air quality).
  • The Sapphire 9000 stud finder provides highly accurate visual indications of stud locations within drywall.
  • The TP-Link TapO camera offers a unique feature of simulating 24/7 recording by taking timed snapshots between motion events on solar power.

Practical Notes

  • Terminal OG Setup: Requires creating a custom dashboard in Home Assistant, and the Terminal app must be configured to take and style snapshots for the e-ink display.
  • Third Reality Garage Door Opener: Requires attaching a tilt sensor to the door and placing an extra garage door remote inside the device. It supports Matter.
  • Zeus Zen32 Implementation: Requires integration with a Z-Wave hub, though it can be used for simple on/off or dimmer functions (up to 3 amps for fans).
  • IKEA Air Quality Sensor Caveats: Settings and time are reset when the device is unplugged; sensor accuracy is lower than premium models. It integrates via Matter.
  • R3 vs FP1E: The Third Reality R3 is recommended for garages due to its extra sensors (RGB, air quality) but is bulkier; the Accara FP1E is suggested for indoor use where aesthetics matter more.
  • Fantic S2 Pro Usage: It can be used for drilling screws in walls and has a locking feature allowing manual tightening/loosening.
  • TP-Link TapO Camera: To achieve near 24/7 coverage, the default 10-second interval snapshot setting must be utilized between motion event recordings; increasing this interval will reduce battery life.
