Autoscout discusses its adoption of AI agents to transform its operations within the automotive marketplace.

OpenAI · 2026-07-07T08:23:39+00:00 · What does an AI-native future look like for one of the world's largest automotive marketplaces?

What This Video Is About

This video discusses Autoscout's strategic adoption of AI agents as a core part of its operational transformation within the global automotive marketplace. The company's overarching goal is to achieve a state where every engineer operates in a hands-off coding environment, relying heavily on integrated code throughout the organization. By pushing this reality forward, Autoscout anticipates exponential improvements not only in the volume of code produced but also in its overall quality.

The discussion centers on how rapidly evolving AI models are forcing fundamental contemplation regarding the future of work and entire industries. The implementation of these agents has already yielded significant efficiency gains for the company.

Detailed Walkthrough

Autoscout's foundational mission is to connect car buyers with sellers across the globe [0:05]. To achieve this, the organization is extensively integrating code throughout its structure, aiming for a state where every engineer can be hands-off coding [0:13]. The company believes that realizing this vision will lead to exponential gains in both the quantity and quality of the code produced [0:26].

A concrete example of this implementation involved developing a "capex agent" shortly after agents were released, which was designed for all product teams [0:35]. This single agent performs an analysis by reviewing several data sources: the teams' calendars, their Slack communications, and their Jira tickets that they have been working on [0:42]. On a weekly basis, this agent pulls out a time schedule detailing where the team spent its hours [0:49]. The impact of this single agent has allowed the entire organization to collectively save approximately one million working hours annually [1:04].

The speaker notes that the models are changing very rapidly; something that functioned correctly three months ago operates inherently differently now [1:11]. This rapid evolution has led to significant contemplation regarding how work and the industry itself will change in an AI-native future [1:31].
