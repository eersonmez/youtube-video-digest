The announcement and demonstration of the new ChatGPT Voice powered by GPT-Live 1.

OpenAI · 2026-07-08T17:23:27+00:00 · This is the new ChatGPT Voice, powered by GPT-Live

What This Video Is About

This video introduces and demonstrates the capabilities of the all-new ChatGPT Voice, which is powered by GPT-Live 1. The core advancement presented is that this voice model functions as a full duplex conversational partner, representing what OpenAI describes as the most powerful voice model ever built. Unlike previous models, it is designed to mimic natural human conversation by accurately following interruptions, pauses, and the inherent flow of thought during dialogue.

The technology moves beyond simple back-and-forth exchanges by integrating advanced reasoning and real-time information retrieval capabilities directly into the conversational interface. Demonstrations show that the voice can tackle complex problem-solving tasks while simultaneously accessing current, up-to-date information from the web—such as checking transit delays or weather forecasts. Furthermore, it introduces sophisticated real-time translation features, allowing for natural conversation across different languages while actively listening to both parties.

Detailed Walkthrough

The presentation begins by establishing the concept of a full duplex voice model [0:19]. This is explained through an analogy comparing it to a normal phone call where one can listen and talk simultaneously [0:37], emphasizing that the new system pushes voice technology closer to real human interaction by accommodating interruptions, pauses, corrections, and the natural flow of thinking aloud [0:50].

The demonstration then shifts focus from conversational fluidity to cognitive capability. The speaker tests whether ChatGPT Voice can reason through harder problems and search the web for current information [1:15]. A scenario is set up where a user needs to fact-check historical dates for an upcoming lecture on the history of sound [1:23]. The model successfully checks specific dates, such as "Scott de Martinville's phone autograph in 1857," "Edison's tinfoil photograph in 1865," and "Berlinger's gramophone in 1887" [1:36]. While performing this check, the model is also tasked with retrieving real-time data regarding delays at the 16th Street BART station and checking for rain in San Francisco [1:46]. The system reports no current advisory for Six Mission and confirms no rain is expected that afternoon [1:56], before correcting one of the user's dates, noting that "Edison's tinfoil photograph should be 1877, not 1865" [2:05].

The demonstration continues by showcasing real-time translation capabilities. A roleplay scenario is initiated where a friend, SJ, is established as a rare book dealer in Paris [2:38]. The system begins live translation into French during the conversation, allowing for natural dialogue between the user and the simulated Parisian dealer regarding pricing and deals [2:42].

Finally, the video concludes by highlighting the model's awareness of when to intervene or remain silent. When a user expresses hunger, the voice responds with recommendations for places to eat based on mood [3:21], demonstrating context-aware assistance.
