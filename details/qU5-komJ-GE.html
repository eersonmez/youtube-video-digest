What This Video Is About

This video details how OpenAI (referred to in the context of the provided transcript as "Omio" based on the title, though the content describes internal OpenAI practices) is leveraging AI tools, specifically Codex and JGPT, to fundamentally transform its product development lifecycle. The central theme is a shift from merely automating existing processes to completely reimagining workflows using available AI capabilities.

The company reports significant gains in efficiency; tasks that previously required three-quarters of a month can now be completed within one month, leading to overall product builds being accomplished in 20 percent of the previous time. This acceleration is driven by integrating these AI models across various functions and engineering roles.

Detailed Walkthrough

The speaker explains that they are building products for their customers on top of AI [0:05]. Every engineer has access to Codex, which they utilize throughout the entire developer lifecycle [0:13]. The observed speed improvement is substantial; tasks previously taking three-quarters of a month can now be completed within one month [0:19]. Consequently, product builds are being executed in 20 percent of the time it used to take [0:28].

The distinction between JGPT and Codex is made clear. JGPT functions as a general assistant for various queries, but when actual work needs to be done, Codex is employed because it makes processes cheaper, faster, and simplifies decision-making by allowing for smaller investments that yield comparable outputs and outcomes [0:36].

Codex's applications are broad, extending beyond simple coding. It can process emails, Slack messages, Jira tickets, summarize information, propose actions, and even draft messages, often with high accuracy [0:51]. OpenAI models have been applied across all these functions. One of the initial steps taken was rolling out JGPT to all employees [1:09].

The core philosophy driving this change is a necessary rethinking of how all functions operate [1:24]. The guiding principle has shifted from asking, "How can I use AI to automate what I have?" to asking, "If we started today with all available AI, how would we rebuild these processes?" This indicates a move toward fundamental process redesign rather than incremental automation [1:30-1:46].
