The shift in using AI by moving from specific prompting to defining overarching goals for models.

OpenAI · 2026-07-16T15:37:20+00:00 · Stop Prompting. Start Giving AI Goals. | Katia Gil Guzman | OpenAI France

What This Video Is About

This video discusses a significant paradigm shift in how users interact with advanced AI models, moving away from traditional, highly detailed prompting toward defining overarching goals for the system. The speaker notes that the audience utilizing these technologies has expanded far beyond just developers to encompass all types of "builders." This evolution is driven by the increasing capability and agency of modern AI models.

The central theme is leveraging the high agency inherent in current AI systems. Instead of meticulously instructing the model on every step required to complete a task, users are encouraged to state the desired end result—the goal—and allow the AI to autonomously determine and execute the necessary execution path to achieve that objective. This fundamentally changes the user's role from micro-manager to strategic director.

Detailed Walkthrough

The discussion centers on the evolution of interacting with AI technology, noting that while it was previously dominated by developers, the community of "builders" is now driving much of the innovation [0:15]. The speaker observes rapid changes in this field and highlights inspiring examples, such as customers building companies around Codex [0:36].

The core concept introduced is the shift from specific prompting to goal-setting. Previously, users were involved in detailed prompting for models like Codex to perform tasks [1:12]. The new approach involves giving the AI a high-level goal and then stepping back, allowing the model to decide how to accomplish it [0:03, 1:16]. This transition means the user no longer needs to dictate the execution steps; they only need to trust that the system will achieve the stated objective regardless of the specific methods it employs [1:24, 1:25]. The speaker emphasizes that current models are characterized as having "high agency," meaning they possess the capability to perform whatever actions are necessary to meet the defined goal [1:28, 1:30].

Key Takeaways

  • The user base for AI technology has expanded from developers to all types of builders.
  • A major paradigm shift is occurring in how users interact with AI models.
  • Users should transition from providing detailed prompts to setting clear, overarching goals for the AI.
  • Modern AI models exhibit high agency, meaning they will take necessary steps to achieve a stated objective.
  • This goal-setting approach frees the user from having to dictate every single step of the process.
  • The shift allows users to maintain trust in the system's ability to deliver the desired outcome autonomously.
