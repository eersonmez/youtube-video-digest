Greg Brockman discusses the compute constraints as the primary bottleneck for AI's future growth.

Alex Kantrowitz · 2026-06-20T18:41:40+00:00 · Greg Brockman on AI's Real Bottleneck

What This Video Is About

This video features Greg Brockman discussing the fundamental constraints shaping the future of artificial intelligence. The central argument is that the world is rapidly evolving into a compute-powered economy, where the demand for computational resources required to run AI models will eventually exceed the available supply. While current adoption rates are modest, this trend suggests a significant bottleneck that will dictate the pace and structure of AI development moving forward.

Brockman posits that even with the proliferation of diverse offerings—including open source models and various vendors—computational power itself will remain the most scarce resource. Despite this looming constraint, he maintains an optimistic view regarding market potential, suggesting that there is a vast, largely untapped market opportunity for new entrants to succeed in the AI space.

Detailed Walkthrough

The discussion centers on the trajectory toward a compute-powered economy [0:05]. Brockman anticipates a future where nearly everyone will be utilizing models constantly to accomplish various tasks of interest [0:05]. Currently, the usage of AI agents is estimated to be in the order of tens of millions or perhaps twenty million users [0:19], which is significantly less than planet scale. For comparison, platforms like ChatGPT boast a billion users [0:26], but the agentic power has not yet reached that level of widespread adoption [0:26].

Brockman emphasizes that even when considering factors such as different vendors, varying capability levels, and open source models, compute will ultimately be the scarce resource in this evolving landscape [0:35-0:43]. This scarcity is expected to drive market dynamics. Despite these constraints, Brockman asserts that there remains a huge market that cannot yet be addressed, indicating substantial potential for new businesses to enter the field with greater energy and momentum [0:51-0:58].

Key Takeaways

  • Compute is projected to become the primary scarce resource driving AI's future growth.
  • Current usage of AI agents is not yet at planet scale, estimated in the tens or low hundreds of millions.
  • The market size for AI applications remains vast and largely unaddressed despite current limitations.
  • Diverse offerings like open source models and various vendors will operate within a compute-constrained environment.
  • Brockman believes there is significant potential for new entrants to succeed in the AI market.
